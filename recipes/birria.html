Birria Tacos

Birria is beef slow-cooked in a stew of peppers, tomatoes and spices, made into a taco and fried in the fat of the stew. While it takes some time for the stew to cook, the actual preparation is pretty simple - a perfect meal for a slow cooker or instapot!

I love topping mine with some fresh pico de gallo and a squeeze of lime juice.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 lb beef shank or similar cut

Marinade

  • 3 dried guajillo peppers or any available dried peppers
  • 1 can of chipotle peppers in adobo
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cumin

Stew

  • 1 medium onion chopped
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 6 whole cloves
  • 1 qt chicken stock

Tacos

  • 4" corn or flour tortillas
  • Any desired toppings

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil, then remove from heat. Soak peppers for 15 minutes. Cut beef into small cubes, season with salt and pepper.
  2. Add all marinade ingredients to a blender. After soaking peppers, remove their stems and seeds, then add to blender. Blend marinade into a paste, then marinate the beef for at least 2 hours.
  3. Saute beef in pan or instant pot. Add 1-2 tbsp of oil and saute onions until translucent.
  4. Add meat, marinade, bay leaves, cinnamon and cloves to the pot. Cover with chicken broth and set to high pressure for 45 minutes. If using a slow cooker or stovetop, set to low heat for 4-6 hours.
  5. When cooking is finished, shred the meat, remove any bones and set aside.
  6. Warm up tortillas then dip them in the stew. Build tacos with meat and desired toppings, then fry over medium heat in nonstick skillet, and enjoy!
